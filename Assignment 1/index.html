Unleashing Power

A Deep Dive into the 2015 Mustang GT Engine Specifications

About Engine

  • Engine Type: 5.0-liter Ti-VCT V8
  • Horsepower: 435 hp @ 6,500 rpm
  • Torque: 400 lb-ft @ 4,250 rpm
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual or optional 6-speed automatic with paddle shifters
  • Fuel Economy (Manual): 15 mpg city / 25 mpg highway
  • Fuel Economy (Automatic): 16 mpg city / 25 mpg highway
  • Bore x Stroke: 92.2 mm x 92.7 mm
  • Compression Ratio: 11.0:1
  • Cooling System: Cross-flow aluminum radiator with dual electric cooling fans
  • Valvetrain: DOHC (Dual Overhead Cam)
  • Ignition System: High-energy coil-on-plug
  • Oil Capacity: Approximately 8 quarts with filter

About Car

When it comes to iconic muscle cars, the Ford Mustang GT stands tall, and the 2015 model is no exception. Under the hood of this powerful machine lies a heart that beats with unbridled strength and precision. Let's delve into the details of the engine specifications that make the 2015 Mustang GT a true powerhouse on the road.

At the core of the Mustang GT's performance is its 5.0-liter Ti-VCT V8 engine. This naturally aspirated beast is designed to deliver a thrilling driving experience, and it does so with impressive numbers. Generating a formidable 435 horsepower at 6,500 rpm, the engine roars to life, ensuring that every acceleration is accompanied by the unmistakable growl of American muscle.

The torque output of the 2015 Mustang GT is equally impressive, boasting 400 lb-ft at 4,250 rpm. This translates to a potent combination of power and responsiveness, giving drivers the confidence to conquer straightaways and curves alike. Whether cruising on the highway or tearing up the track, the Mustang GT's V8 engine is a force to be reckoned with.

About Car In Details

Moving beyond the raw power figures, it's essential to explore the engine's internal specifications. The bore x stroke dimensions of 92.2 mm x 92.7 mm showcase a balance between efficiency and performance. With a compression ratio of 11.0:1, the engine maximizes power output while maintaining optimal fuel efficiency.

The Mustang GT's valvetrain is managed by a sophisticated Dual Overhead Cam (DOHC) system. This design allows for precise control over the intake and exhaust valves, optimizing airflow and enhancing overall engine performance. Complementing this, the Sequential Multiport Electronic Fuel Injection system ensures that fuel is delivered with accuracy, contributing to both power and fuel efficiency.
